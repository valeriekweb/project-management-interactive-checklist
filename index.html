PM Coordination Checklist

You may not own the work, but you do own making sure it keeps moving.

★Be proactive.
✉️Offer to create and send updates.
🧭Keep the team aligned before anyone has to ask.
1

When Scheduling Anything

Meetings, milestones, deadlines
📅
  • Confirm date + day of week
  • Confirm time + timezone
  • Check for conflicts with key attendees
  • Add clear title + purpose
  • Attach agenda or expected outcome
  • Invite the right people only
  • Set a reminder buffer
💡Slow down for 10 seconds before sending invites. That tiny pause prevents stupid calendar crimes.
2

Before / After Major Milestones

🚩
  • Ask who is on PTO in the next 1 to 2 weeks
  • Confirm team availability and capacity reset
  • Identify immediate follow-up work
  • Flag risks that appear right after completion
  • Send a quick “what just happened” summary
⭐Don’t wait to be asked. Milestone done means PTO check and summary go out automatically.
3

Closing a Project or Major Phase

🏁
  • Schedule the retrospective immediately
  • Capture what went well
  • Capture what did not
  • Capture what to change next time
  • Confirm owners for follow-up actions
  • Acknowledge team effort
❤️Offer the retro before anyone reminds you. That’s the difference between leading and reacting.
4

Weekly Rhythm Check

🔄
  • Review all active projects and statuses
  • Check timelines slipping silently
  • Confirm priorities have not changed
  • Look for blocked tasks
  • Nudge owners before things escalate
  • Update tracking tools to match reality
🎯Your weekly reset is where future chaos either starts or gets prevented. Irritatingly powerful habit.
5

Before Any Meeting You Run

👥
  • Ask why this meeting exists
  • Define the desired outcome
  • Prepare the key questions in advance
  • Identify who actually needs to speak
  • Kill the meeting if it should have been a message
⚡No purpose, no meeting. Humans adore calendars full of nonsense, so you’ll have to resist for them.
6

After Any Meeting

📝
  • Send summary, decisions, and next steps
  • Assign clear owners, not “the team”
  • Set deadlines
  • Update tracking tools immediately
✉️Close the loop fast. If it isn’t written down, people will remember three different fake versions.
7

Communication Reality Check

💬
  • Did stakeholders get status, risks, and changes?
  • Are you assuming people know things you never said out loud?
  • Is anything ambiguous that will bite later?
📣Over-communicate a little now so you don’t spend next week doing archaeological digs through Slack.
8

Be Proactive: Offer Updates

📨
  • Create and send updates regularly
  • Do not wait to be asked for a summary
  • Share progress, risks, decisions, and next steps
  • Use a simple, consistent format
  • Tailor updates to the audience
🛫People trust the PM who surfaces information before anyone starts wondering what’s going on.
9

Pre-“I’m Done” for the Day Check

📋
  • Look at tomorrow’s meetings and prep gaps
  • Check any deadlines you are pretending feel fine
  • Capture loose thoughts before they vanish
  • Close the loop on one lingering item
🌙Set up tomorrow before logging off. It’s dull, but so is waking up confused.
